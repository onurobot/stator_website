Why we're called Stator

In an electric motor, the stator is the part that never moves — a fixed ring of copper windings that captures wild electromagnetic energy and channels it into precise, usable motion.

The still frame that directs the storm.

That is exactly what we do for AI.

Your stack of AI tools is the rotor — fast, powerful, and spinning out of control. Stator is the stationary control layer wrapped around it, converting unpredictable spend into directed, measurable output. The energy stays; the chaos doesn't.
